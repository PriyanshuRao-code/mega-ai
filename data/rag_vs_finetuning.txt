RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) vs Fine-Tuning
==================================================

Overview:
The debate between RAG and Fine-tuning is central to modern LLM application design. 

RAG:
- Pros: Access to real-time data, verifiable citations, no retraining cost.
- Cons: High retrieval latency, dependent on retrieval quality (retrieval-precision bottleneck).
- Use Case: Knowledge bases, dynamic documentation, multi-hop reasoning.

Fine-Tuning:
- Pros: Domain adaptation, behavior steering (style/tone), reduced prompt length.
- Cons: Static knowledge (knowledge cut-off), expensive to update, "black box" reasoning.
- Use Case: Specific jargon adoption, unique formatting requirements, offline tasks.

Comparison:
RAG is generally preferred for knowledge-intensive tasks, while Fine-tuning is superior for task-specific behavioral alignment.
Many production systems now use a hybrid approach: a fine-tuned model optimized for RAG-driven context.
